Computer Network - The Network Edge

Hosts are divided into two categories: clients and server

  • clients: desktop, laptops, smartphones, and so on.
  • servers: powerful machines that store and distribute Web pages, stream videos, relay email, and so on.
  • data centers: stores most of the servers from which we receive search results, email, Web pages, videos, and so on.

1️⃣ Access Networks

  • edge router: the network that physically connects an end system to the first router.

Home Access: DSL, Cable, FTTH, and 5G Fixed Wireless

Two most prevalent types of broadband residential access today: digital subscriber line(DSL) and cable

  • digital subscriber line(DSL): uses the existing telephone line exchange data with a digital subscriber line access multiplexer(DLAM)

The residential telephone line carries both data and traditional telephone signals

  • A high-speed downstream channel: 50 kHz ~ 1 MHz
  • A medium-speed upstream channel: 4 kHz ~ 50 kHz
  • An ordinary two-way telephone channel: 0 ~ 4 kHz

  • cable Internet access: makes use of the television company’s existing cable television infrastructure.

The downstream channel and upstream channel all depends on how many users are active, because users in the same network share a single channel.

  • fiber to the home(FTTH): an up-and-coming technology that provides even higher speed.
    • FTTH provide an optical fiber path from the CO directly to the home and allow gigabits per second range.

There are two competing optical-distribution network architectures: active optical networks(AONs) and passive optical networks(PONs)

  • active optical networks(AON): switched Ethernet.
  • passive optical networks(PONs): Figure 1.7 shows FTTH using PON distribution architecture.
    • Each home has optical network terminator(ONT)
    • The ONTs are connected by dedicated optical fiber to a neighborhood splitter and combines them into a single shared optical fiber

  • 5G fixed wireless: uses beam-forming technology and send data wirelessly from a provider’s base station to the a modem at home.

Access in the Enterprise (and the Home): Ethernet and WiFi

  • Ethernet: twisted-pair copper wire to connect to an Ethernet switch.
  • WiFi: wireless LAN access based on IEEE 802.11 technology.
    • 802.11 today provides a shared transmission rate of up to < 100 Mbps.

2️⃣ Physical Media

  • physical medium: allows data to be sent by propagating electromagnetic waves or optical pulses across.
  • There are two types of physical media: guided media and unguided media
    • guided media: the waves are guided along a solid medium such as a fiber-optic cable, a twisted-pair copper wire, or a coaxial cable
    • unguided media: the waves propagate in the atmosphere and in outer space such as in a wireless LAN or digital satellite channel.

Twisted-Pair Copper Wire

Twisted-pair copper wire is the least expensive and most commonly used guided transmission medium.

  • Unshielded twisted pair(UTP): commonly used for computer networks within a building, LANs for instance.
    • Range 10 Mbps - 10 Gbps, depending on the thickness of the wire and the distance between the transmitter and receiver.

Coaxial Cable

Coaxial cable is commonly used for television systems, which has been coupled with cable modems to provide Internet access.

  • shared medium: more than one receiver are hooked up to the transmitter.
    • In this case, transmitter shifts the digital signal to a specific frequency band which different end systems can receive.

Fiber Optics

Optics fiber is thin and flexible medium that conduct pulses of light, with each pulse representing a bit. It also is the prevalent in the backbone of the Internet, but it is expensive. Its speed range 51.8 Mbps to 39.8 Gbps.

Terrestrial Radio Channels

Terrestrial radio channels carry signals in the electromagnetic spectrum. They do not require physical wire to be installed.

Satellite Radio Channels

Satellite links two or more Earth-based microwave transmitter/receiver.

  • geostationary satellites: permanently remains above the same spot on Earth. It is often used in areas without access to DSL or cable-based Internet access, but have substantial signal propagation delay of 280 ms.
  • low-earth orbiting(LEO): placed much closer to Earth compared to geostationary satellites and do not remain permanently above one spot on Earth. It could be the technology for Internet access in the future.
