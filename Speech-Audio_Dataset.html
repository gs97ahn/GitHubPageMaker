Speech - Audio Dataset

Audio Dataset

Dataset

  • Traning data for machine learning
  1. Manual Recording
    • Use audio equipment for manual recording
    • Level of preparation is hardest

Caution when Recording Manually

  • Quality of microphone and recorder
  • Recording format
    • wav format. 44.1k / more than 16bits
  • Volume
    • As loud as possible with no distortion
  • Noise
    • Get rid of noise as much as possible
    • Use a large shotgun mic for directivity
  1. Internet Collection
    • Download it from the internet and manufacture
    • (ex) freesound.org - More convienet than manual recording - Diversified audio quality - Deflective data
  2. Public Dataset
    • Public dataset provided by someone else
    • Most convinent
    • Audio data you want might not be there
    • Audio dataset are verified
    • https://paperswithcode.com/datasets?task=audio-classification&page=1

Making My Own Dataset

  • Choose the most similar public dataset + Music(recorded or downloaded from internet) to it = My Own Dataset

Public Audio Dataset

AudioSet

  • 632 audio event classes
  • 2,084,320 human-labeled 10-second sound clips drawn from YouTube videos
  • The same sound could be annotated as different labels

ESC-50

  • 2000 environment audio recording from Freesound.org
  • 5 sec-clips of 50 different classes

UrbanSound8K

  • 8732 (<=4s) of urban sounds taken from freesound.org
  • 10 classes

Caution when using UrbanSound8K

  1. Don’t reshuffle the data! Use the predefined 10 folds and perform 10-fold (not 5-fold) cross validation
  2. Don’t evaluate just on one split! Use 10-fold (not 5-fold) cross validation and average the scores

K-fold cross validation

  • Split the entire dataset into K and learn it K times and get average recognition rate

Reference

  • Acoustics Lecture by Wongeun Oh in The Acoustical Society of Korea
