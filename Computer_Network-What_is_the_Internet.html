Computer Network - What is the Internet

1️⃣ A Nuts-and-Bolts Description

  • The Internet: A computer network that interconnects billions of computing devices.
    • Today, approximately half of the world’s population are active mobile Internet users.
    • By 2025, users in the Internet is expected to increase upto 75%.
  • Hosts or End system: Devices that have Internet connection.

  • Communication links and Packet switches: Network that connects end systems together.
  • Transmission rate: Rate that different links can transmit data, measured in bits/seconds.
  • Packets: The resulting packages of information that are sent through the network to the destination end system, where they are reassembled into the original data.
  • Routers and Link-layer switches: Two most prominent types of packet switches. They forward packets toward their ultimate destinations.
  • Route or Path: The sequence of communication links and packet switches traverse.
  • Internet Service Providers(ISPs): Provide a variety of types of network access to the end systems.
    • Each ISP network, whether upper-tier or lower-tier, is managed independently, runs th IP protocol, and conforms to certain naming and address conventions.
  • Protocols: Control the sending and receiving of information within the Internet.
  • Transmission Control Protocol(TCP) and Internet Protocol(IP): Two of the most important protocols in the Internet.
    • IP: Specifies the format of the packets that are sent and received among routers and end systems.
    • TCP/IP: The Internet’s principal protocols.
  • Internet standards: A standard that everyone agree on what each and every protocol does.
  • Requests for comments(RFCs): Standards documents that the Internet Engineering Task Force(IETF) developed.
    • There are currently nearly 9000 RFCs that define protocols such as TCP, IP, HTTP(for the Web), and SMTP(for e-mail).

2️⃣ A Services Description

  • Distributed applications: Multiple end systems that exchange data with each other.
    • Internet applications run on end systems and NOT in packet switches in network core.
  • Socket interface: Set of rules that the sending program must follow so that the Internet can deliver the data to the destination program.
    • This is similar to the postal service, which you need to follow certain rules such as putting the stamp, having the sender’s and receiver’s postal code, address, and etc.

3️⃣ What is a Protocol?

Human Analogy

Computer network protocols are similar to how humans interact.

Network Protocols

Similar to human protocols, network protocols communicate through “wire” by controlling the flow of bits.

A protocol defines the format and the order of messages exchanged between two or more communicating entities, as well as the actions taken on the transmission and/or receipt of a message or other event.
